Long Live Juice WRLD

Jarad Anthony Higgins, professionally known as Juice WRLD, was born and raised in Chicago on December 2nd 1998, and started making a name for himself in rap in highschool. He released his first album "Goodbye & Good Riddance," in 2018, with Grade A Productions, who he was signed to. This album is home to one of his most popular songs titled "Lucid Dreams," which actually was certified Diamond, which means it has over 10 million streams/sales. He also said that he wrote that song in less than 30 minutes, and didn't think it would get as popular as it did.

He then released his sophomore album, "Death Race For Love," in 2019, which he wrote in less than a week, and is about 72 minutes long, which is quite long for a rap album. This was the last album he released before his death on December 8th, 2019, due to an accidental drug overdose.

Freestyling

As I previously mentioned, Juice has said to have written most of his songs pretty quickly, because he didn't actually write as much as he fresstyled to the beats, and then made a song from that, or even released some songs that were full freestyles. He has been labeled as one of the best freestyle rappers in rap, and there are many youtube videos showcasing this, including one where he raps for an hour straight, which is very impressive, as most rappers cannot go that long.

This is how he made so many songs so quickly, but still managed to make them so good, because this was just his talent, to be able to come up with raw, emotionally, witty rhymes and lines on the spot, is something a lot of people cannot do well. He has said that he has over 2,000 songs recorded, and there are only so many that are actually released in his albums. This is why there is a whole community of people sharing his unreleased songs, and why there will still be a lot of content of his to look forward to, even though he has already passed.

Before I found my love for Juice, I would occasionally fresstyle with my boyfriend and his old friends, but I never really got into until I listen to Juice and wanted to try and improve my freestyle rapping. I have gotten all of my friends to freestyle with me to beats on youtube, and although we are not perfect, It is a lot of fun, and it is so satisfying to see improvement and play with it. Sometimes we try and rap over instrumental songs we like of varying styles, and we even try and bounce off of eachother.
